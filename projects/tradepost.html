Tradepost | UX/UI Research & Design

Tradepost is a concept for an app that aims to provide a quick and easy way for users to buy and sell their furniture. This project seeks to provide a user-to-user resale app for furniture. With this in mind, the app was designed with the goals, motivations, needs, and pain points of furniture buyers in mind.

Defining the problem.

On Tradepost, users can buy and sell furniture with other users. This project was inspired by my own experiences buying and selling furniture on Facebook Marketplace and Craigslist. Although I was still able to accomplish my goals with these websites, I found myself experiencing a lot of frustration, especially with encountering dishonest sellers and even scammers at times. It was also difficult to find the specific items that I wanted because they’re not specialized for furniture. Inspired by my own experiences, I designed Tradepost to be a marketplace that’s specifically catered to buying and selling furniture.

Competitor Analysis

For the competitor analysis, I analyzed OfferUp and Depop. Offerup is a resale market with an emphasis on in-person transactions. Depop is a popular clothing resale market that implements features of social media applications. I decided to evaluate OfferUp because it is one of the largest resale markets and Depop because of it’s large cultural reach and it’s success in tapping into new trends among consumers. These two applications are opposite in terms of their execution. OfferUp is very utilitarian and plain, while Depop is bold and trendy. I wanted to study what aspects these applications got right, and what aspects they got wrong. In order to examine the usability of these applications, I conducted a heuristic evaluation in order to identify usability problems. I found that OfferUp has issues with its layout of images as well as the placement of ads. Depop has issues with its efficiency of use.

Competitor Usability Test

In addition, I also conducted a usability test with OfferUp. Based on the findings from my heuristic evaluation, I hoped to evaluate the usability of the app by conducting pilot usability testing. I also hoped to test the overall effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction that the app does or does not provide. I wanted to uncover any issues that I may have missed in my heuristic evaluation. The usability test confirmed and also revealed several issues. It confirmed some of the things that I had suspected would cause issues, such as the disruptive ads and the difficulty in unlisting items. It also revealed several other issues, such as the lack of system status while searching as far as shipping vs. pickup method, and the fact that notifications persist in the inbox even after they’re resolved, which can create confusion.

User Research

In order to better understand the motivation, needs, and behaviors of users, I conducted two user research studies involving interviewing and participatory observation. During the interview, my goal was to gain an understanding of user habits, how users utilize online market places to meet their goals, and what pain points they commonly face. During the participatory observation, I observed a user performing two activities: searching and selecting an item, and listing an item. The purpose of this activity was to understand their process and the logic behind their decision making.

The interview confirmed to me that facilitating trustworthiness between buyers and sellers is important. This is especially relevant because most resale markets do not allow refunds, so buyers must have confidence in sellers. An aspect that I hadn’t thought of before is that feedback and interaction can encourage sellers to stay on the platform. Platforms that do not have likes or other methods of interaction may be less beneficial, since sellers may be more likely to become discouraged.

Based on these findings, I identified several features that would address certain needs and pain points of users:

  • Filters–Allow users to filter by type, color, condition, price, and location.
  • Simple listing process–A simple listing process with pre-set categories and the ability to copy prior listings would make listing easier and more convenient.
  • User profiles and reviews–his creates greater trustworthiness.

UX Storytelling

The purpose of UX Storytelling is to help designers understand and empathize with the users of their products. UX Storytelling involves taking user data and repackaging it in a way that makes it clear and memorable, allowing UX designers to more effectively communicate insights from research and see things from users' perspective. It can be difficult to understand insights from raw data or bullet points — UX Storytelling instead communicates this information in a way that is memorable, easier to understand, and more likely to resonate, because storytelling is a powerful tool for communication. In this portion, I used UX storytelling as a way to understand how users use online resale markets.

Based on the results of the prior research, I created two personas and empathy maps with the goal of understanding users' goals, needs, motivations, and pain points.

Wireframes

Using my prior research to inform me, I designed several user flows, include searching and filtering, viewing user profiles and reviews, making offers, and favoriting items.

Usability Test

In order to assess the usability of the design, I conducted a virtual usability test with the low-fidelity prototype. The participant was asked to complete a series of tasks:

  • Search for an item and filter the search results.
  • View your offers and show how to accept or deny them.
  • View the items that you favorited.
  • View reviews of a seller.

High-Fidelity Design

For the high-fidelity design, I paid special attention to aspects such as design consistency, accessibility, and visual hierarchy. When designing the visual aesthetic of the app, my goal was to create a clean and cohesive interface design. I used a fairly limited color palette in order to prevent the interface's color from clashing with photos. The primary fonts are Montserrat and Helvetica Neue, with varying font weights. In order to ensure readability, I conducted contrast testing.

Evaluation

Following this, I conducted an impression test to assess users' immediate reaction to the design. This study was conducted virtually. The participant was given a link to view the wireframes, and asked about their initial reaction and thoughts. They were also asked to choose words to describe the app.

In addition, I conducted a cognitive walkthrough to assess the usability of the app with a different participant. The participant was asked to walkthrough a series of tasks, including:

  • Search for an item and filter the search results.
  • View and accept offers.
  • Make a listing.

Based on the results of the impression test and cognitive walkthrough, the high fidelity prototype was successful in conveying the purpose and features offered by the application.

Conclusion

The most important takeaway was the importance of empathizing and seeing things from the perspective of users, rather than only relying on my own judgements. This was sometimes difficult because as a designer, I am used to following my own instincts. By getting feedback from users, whether it be from user research, usability testing, impression testing, and more, I was able to discover weaknesses in the product and gain insight on how to improve the overall experience of users.
