Washerly | Concept & Lo-Fi Design

An On-Demand Laundry Service App

Defining the Problem

For the busy student or professional, it can be hard to carve out time to do laundry every week. The process of washing, drying, and folding laundry is often time-consuming and inconvenient. How do we create a more convenient and stress-free laundry experience?

The Goal

My goal was to design an on-demand laundry app where users can arrange for their laundry to be picked up, washed, folded, and then delivered back. I wanted to create a simple and straight-forward experience, without unnecessary bells and whistles.

Prior Research

In order to understand the problem better, I conducted interviews with potential users to understand their needs, habits, and pain points related to laundry and cleaning services. Based on these interviews, I identified several common pain points:

  • Time–Sorting, washing, drying, and folding laundry is time-consuming.
  • Inconvenience–Visiting a laundromat can be inconvenient and laundry can be heavy and cumbersome.
  • Quality & Consistency–Clothes can get damaged or shrink if not washed correctly.
  • Scheduling & Coordination: It can be difficult to schedule time for dropping off and picking up laundry.

User Personas

Based on my prior research, I created user personas in order to more effectively identify demographics, behaviors, needs, and pain points.

User Flow

In order to understand the most important paths and actions that users will take, I mapped out the user flow. This helped identify the critical touchpoints and how to structure the content and navigation to support these actions.

Site Map

With a clear understanding of the user flow, I created a sitemap that organizes the content and pages to support the flow. This provides a hierarchical structure of all the pages and how they are interconnected.

Wireframe Ideating

With the site map defined, I sketched wireframes to help explore ideas, improve usability, and guide the design. I performed several iterations based on feedback and exploration.
