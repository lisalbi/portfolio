Wazo | UX/UI Research & Design

A mental health platform aimed towards college students.

Defining the problem

One in three college students report struggling with serious depression. Moreover, many college campuses are ill-equipped to address these concerns. College students often struggle to get the help they need due to money concerns as well as stigma.

The Goal

Wazo seeks to bridge these gaps by offering a free, anonymous mentorship service that pairs college students with mentors.

Team

Project Management: Noam Grebler Farras, Yewon Kim
Development: Marshall Briggs, John Tran
Product Design: Amanda Wu, Vanessa Ta, and Me

My Responsibilities

User Flows, User Wireframes, Prototypes, Market Research

Process

Conceptualizing Ideas

The central concept for the app is that students are matched with a mentor according to their needs. The matching process begins with a pairing form, which assesses the individual needs and personal preferences of the user. This allows for us to match users with the mentor that best suits their specific needs. To begin with, we started with a simplified user flow and site map:

Wireframing

The central consideration for the wireframes was creating a user-centric and accessible experience. Each feature was a collaborative effort, in which we each designed wireframes as individuals, then came together and critiqued and combined ideas. After coming to a final consensus on wireframe designs, we moved onto designing higher fidelity prototypes.

Final Designs

In designing the prototypes, we wanted to emphasize an approachable and straightforward interface. In addition, we wanted our design to highlight core values of Wazo, such as inclusivity, anonymity, and non-judgement.
