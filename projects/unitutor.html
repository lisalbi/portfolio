Unitutor | Design Exercise

An app that connects users to tutors.

Defining the Problem

As a college student, I noticed that many tutoring apps aimed aimed towards students had designs that were outdated and confusing to use. As an exercise in design, I decided to design a tutoring app with a focus on user-centered design as well as modern, well-branded design.

Considerations

In order to design a user-friendly, efficient, and visually appealing interface, I had several key considerations:

  • User-Centered Design–knowing the users and their needs, preferences, behaviors, and pain points.
  • Layout and Navigation–Organizing content and pages logically to create a clear and intuitive structure.
  • Accessibility–Using sufficient color contrast and readable font sizes to ensure text is legible.
  • Visual Hierarchy–Use size, color, and positioning to emphasize important elements and guide users’ attention.
  • Consistency & Branding–Use a consistent color scheme, typography, and design elements across all screens and components.
